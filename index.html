About the Competion!

The Namibian Annual High School Programming Competition is an annual event designed to encourage and promote Programming skills among high school students in Namibia. It provides a platform for students to showcase their programming talents, compete with their peers, and learn from industry professionals.

F.A.Q

  • What is NAHPC?

    The Namibian Annual High School Programming Competition NAHPC is an annual event designed to encourage and promote Programming skills among high school learners in Namibia. It provides a platform for learners to showcase their programming talents, compete with their peers, and learn from industry professionals.

  • Who can participate in the competition?

    The competition is open to all high/secondary school learners in Namibia. Participants must be enrolled in a Namibian high/Secondary school and have computer science as a subject. Teams can be formed by learners from the same school.

  • How can I register my school for the competition?

    To register your school for the competition, visit the "Register Your School" section on our website. Fill out the registration form with the required details, including school name, contact information, and the names of participating students. Once submitted, you will receive a confirmation email with further instructions.

  • What programming languages are allowed in the competition?

    Participants will use Python as the programming language for the competition. This decision ensures a standardized platform for all competitors and allows for a more focused assessment of Programming skills.

  • Are there any prizes for the winners?

    Yes, there are exciting prizes for the top-performing teams and individuals. Details about the prizes will be announced closer to the event date.

  • When and where will the competition be held?

    The competition will take place at the IUM Dorado Main Campus in Windhoek. The exact date and time of the event will be announced on our website and communicated to all registered participants via email.
